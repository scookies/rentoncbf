Our Story

The Beginning (2018)

It all started with a simple observation: children are natural entrepreneurs. They see opportunities everywhere, create solutions from nothing, and approach challenges with unbounded creativity. The Renton Children's Business Fair was born from the belief that these natural instincts should be nurtured, not suppressed.

Our founders, a group of local educators and business leaders, recognized that traditional education often lacks practical business experience. They envisioned a platform where children could learn by doing â€“ where abstract concepts become tangible through real customer interactions and actual sales.

Growing Impact (2019-2021)

What began as a small community event quickly grew into something extraordinary. Our first fair welcomed 25 young entrepreneurs. By 2021, we were hosting multiple events throughout the year, each attracting over 100 participants and thousands of visitors.

The transformation we witnessed was remarkable. Shy children became confident presenters. Creative ideas evolved into sustainable business concepts. Most importantly, we saw young people develop critical life skills: communication, financial literacy, problem-solving, and resilience.

Expanding Horizons (2022-Present)

Today, the Renton Children's Business Fair has become a model for youth entrepreneurship programs. We've expanded beyond traditional markets to include technology showcases, sustainability initiatives, and social impact projects. Our alumni have gone on to start real businesses, win entrepreneurship competitions, and pursue business education with newfound passion.

We've also developed mentorship programs, connecting young entrepreneurs with local business leaders who provide guidance, support, and real-world insights. Our community of supporters has grown to include parents, teachers, local businesses, and civic organizations, all united in their commitment to empowering the next generation.

Our Vision

A Future of Empowered Entrepreneurs

We envision a world where every child has the opportunity to explore their entrepreneurial potential, where young minds are celebrated for their creativity and innovation, and where business skills are as fundamental as reading and writing.

01

Regional Expansion

Establish Children's Business Fairs in every major community across the Pacific Northwest, creating a network of young entrepreneurs.

02

Educational Integration

Partner with schools to integrate entrepreneurship education into standard curriculum, making business literacy accessible to all students.

03

Innovation Hub

Create a year-round innovation center where young entrepreneurs can access resources, mentorship, and collaborative workspace.

04

Develop programs that address global challenges, empowering youth to create businesses that solve real-world problems.
